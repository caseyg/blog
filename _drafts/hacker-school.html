Emily Sommer sent some excellent questions from Hacker School that (with her permission) I'm posting my replies to as a case study of how to approach one's first open source project. I had suggested

First up: SVG-edit, which is something I've been looking for since I first learned how to use Adobe Illustrator (6 years ago). [I found this yesterday and was simply ecstatic that it exists as a web app that I can use, then I realized it was built in JavaScript and mildly freaked out in excitement.] It appears to be open to contributors, has an MIT license, and is hosted by Google Code, so I think it's open source. How can I know for sure? It also seems to have a fairly small yet active community. Finally, since it is built mostly in JavaScript I could EASILY contribute (okay, perhaps not immediately easily, but certainly more easily than I could contribute to something like Inkscape, which is built in C++ and will not install on my computer).

 

Here's the (brainstormed, off-the-top-of-my-head-last-week) list of things I'm checking.
Can this person independently navigate the resources my project has on the website? (gauging base level initiative and independence)
Is this person trying to do something that would be helpful to me? (legitimate peripheral participation)
Does the email have a concrete, testable goal/outcome in mind?
Are they asking for help on something specific? (if I don't realize they need help, I probably won't give it)
Is there a way I can publicly respond to this person? (so others can learn from it and help with it, and I won't be a bottleneck)

Second: ED-design labs. They are a community of educators and technologists working together to make experimental, free (I think) educational software for ipads and the web. I found them through Code Montage (seems like a cool resource). Here is their github account (full of cool projects), but nothing has been updated in two months and more. While looking at their website, I recognized an old friend from college as one of the teachers involved, so I might shoot him an email as a starting point. Again, though, is 2 months too abandoned? And how do I know for sure if they are open source?
Those are the three projects that I think would be cool to contribute to. Any feedback you can give would be much appreciated. Thanks Mel! Again, so cool to meet you and work with you a bit last week,
