Because I am really bad at being formal, cough and because I rarely hear anyone pray the way I do (most folks seem to have a better memory for Scripture verses, grammar rules, and basic eloquence) -- here's something recent from my prayer journal. This is the sort of conversation I'd have with God when I was little and snuck out of church to go climb trees.

Hey.

So it seems I've got this talent. And I'd like to cultivate it. (Help.)

I will cultivate it in silence if that's how it ought to be, or under scrutiny of visiblity if that's how it ought to be. I will learn it faster than I think I can, or slower than I think I have the patience for. I will hold impatience back, or shower effort out beyond the lifeblood that I think I have in me -- let this not be about the thing I want, but rather what should be and what is best.

But I need discipline to cultivate this into a mature flexibility, and for the right way forward to be (at least initially) very loud and hit-over-the-head-with-frying-pan obvious. Seriously. Thick skull here. And I'd like guidance and companionship, clearly given, consciously acknowledged -- but hey; whatever works. Whatever ought to be. I'll roll with it as best I can.

Thanks.

(And at this point in the conversation, I'd shut up and listen, and occasionally laugh.)
